The Biography of Yossele Rosenblatt

Regarded as the best cantor of his time

  • Yossele was born on May 8, 1882 in Bila Tserkva, Russian Empire now Ukraine
  • he came from a long line of cantors and started out in a kids choir and they quickly called him wunderkind which means child prodigy and that's when his solo career was launched
  • He moved at age 7 to Sadigura, Austria
  • At 17 he became a professional cantor in Vienna followed by a tour from the Austro-Hungarian Empire
  • When he was 18 he married Taube Kaufman and took a full time position in Munkatch, Hungary
  • In 1901 he moved to Pressburg
  • Five Years later he took a position in Hamburg, Germany
  • In 1912 he moved to Harlem, New York to take a position in Ohev Tzedek
  • In August 1927 he left and went on a tour
  • This tour started on the high holy days in Chicago then in Detroit and continued after to Minneapolis, Seattle, Indianapolis, Columbus, Milwakee, Philledalphia, and Washington, DC
  • In Washington,DC he met then president Calvin Coolidge
  • In 1927 he signed a 10 year contract with the Anshei Sfard Congregation
  • Rosenblatt's fame extended beyond the Jewish world earning him large concert fees, a singing role in the 1927 film The Jazz Singer, and the sobriquet "The Jewish Caruso"
  • In the early 1920s he invested in a jewish newspaper that turned out to be unsuccesful which he invested most of his money causing him to file for bankcruptcy in 1925 and he vowed to pay back his debts anyways
  • He was offered a job in a film in Israel and he took his family to israel
  • He died of a heart attack at the age of 51 on June 19, 1933 in Jerusalem
  • They say he had the greatest influence on cantorial music's golden age
